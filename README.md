> **Geo-BC** is a large-scale, preference-aligned benchmark for **fine-grained reasoning** and **spatiotemporal geo-world understanding** in remote sensing.  
> It moves beyond conventional change captioning by evaluating whether models can perform **structured reasoning**, **rule-constrained semantic filtering** and **uncertainty-aware decision making**.

## 📌 Overview

Remote sensing change captioning has made strong progress in describing visible differences between bi-temporal images, but most existing benchmarks mainly reward **surface-level descriptions** such as “new buildings appeared” or “vegetation decreased.” They provide limited support for evaluating whether a model can reason over spatial evidence, apply semantic rules, reject pseudo-changes, and align predictions with geographic expert judgment. Geo-BC is designed to fill this gap. 

Geo-BC reframes bi-temporal change analysis from **free-form caption generation** to **structured change interpretation**. Instead of only asking *what changed*, Geo-BC asks whether a model can:

- localize relevant evidence,
- reason over spatial and semantic relations,
- determine whether the change is valid under a land-use taxonomy,
- filter out task-irrelevant variation,
- and calibrate confidence under ambiguity.

## 🧠 What makes Geo-BC different?

To move beyond answer-only supervision, we introduce a process reward modeling scheme that aligns fine-grained reasoning trajectories with geographically grounded preferences. By decoupling evaluation into visual grounding, spatiotemporal logic, standards compliance, and confidence calibration, Geo-BC supports preference correction at the trajectory level rather than only final-answer selection.

Unlike traditional remote sensing change captioning datasets, Geo-BC is designed around **structured multimodal reasoning**. Each sample requires the model to follow a six-step reasoning process:

1. **Global perception**  
2. **Instance-level visual grounding**  
3. **Spatial relation modeling**  
4. **Semantic reasoning**  
5. **Future inference**  
6. **Confidence correction** 

In addition, Geo-BC introduces a **positive-negative dual-track reasoning framework**:

- **Positive track**: verifies whether a candidate change is supported by visual, spatial, and temporal evidence
- **Negative track**: checks whether the observed difference should be filtered out as non-target or intra-class variation under the operational land-use taxonomy

This makes Geo-BC much closer to real-world Earth observation workflows than standard captioning benchmarks.

## 🏗️ Benchmark Construction

Geo-BC is built with a **judge-supervised, preference-aligned pipeline**. Candidate reasoning trajectories are generated by multiple multimodal models **(Gemini 2.5 pro, Chatgpt 4.1, LLaVA-OneVision-1.5, QVQ, Qwen2.5-vl-72b)** and then evaluated by a **decoupled mixture-of-judges** system:

- **Judge 1: Visual-Language Expert Gemini 3.1 pro**  
  evaluates global perception and visual grounding
- **Judge 2: Spatiotemporal Logic Expert Deepseek R1**  
  evaluates spatial relation modeling, reasoning, and future inference
- **Judge 3: Standards-Compliance Judge Chatgpt 4o**  
  evaluates taxonomy/rule compliance, uncertainty handling, and final answer validity

The paper reports **94.0% agreement** between AI-selected winning candidates and human preference judgments from six evaluators with remote-sensing backgrounds.

## 📊 Dataset Statistics

### Full Benchmark
- **109,224** spatiotemporal image samples
- Derived from **GF-1, GF-2, GF-6, and GF-7** observations
- **327,672** inference trajectories
- **382,284** secondary evaluation statements
- Covers **14** land-use categories
- Positive-to-negative ratio: approximately **1:4**

### Geo-BC Pro
- **39,646** manually curated challenging samples
- Designed for stress-testing fine-grained reasoning and semantic filtering
- Positive-to-negative ratio: **1:1** 

### Data Split
- Train / Val / Test = **7 / 1 / 2**
- Geographic non-overlap enforced to avoid spatial leakage

## 🧪 Benchmark Tasks

Geo-BC includes a **three-stage benchmark suite**:

### Stage I: Basic Change Discrimination
Given only a bi-temporal image pair, the model predicts:
- whether meaningful change occurred,
- the pre-change category,
- the post-change category.

**Metrics**:
- CDA
- CC-L1
- CC-L2

### Stage II: Controlled Rationale Generation
Given a target region and its label, the model generates an attribution rationale.

**Metrics**:
- VE (Visual Evidence)
- LE (Logical Evidence)
- CE (Conclusion Evidence)
- LHR (Logical Hypothesis Reliability) 

### Stage III: Template-Guided Autonomous Deep Interpretation
The model performs end-to-end structured interpretation using the reasoning template.

**Metrics**:
- VP (Visual Perception)
- SL (Spatiotemporal Logic)
- RA (Rule Application)
- DD (Domain Depth)
- CC (CoT Coherence)

## 📈 Baseline Results

The paper benchmarks six representative models:

- QVQ-Max
- Qwen2.5-VL-7B-Instruct
- InternVL3-8B
- LLaVA-OneVision
- MiMo-V2-Flash
- SkySense :contentReference[oaicite:17]{index=17}

Key findings:

- **SkySense** achieves the best Stage I change detection accuracy (**78.74 CDA**), showing strong low-level remote-sensing perception.
- **Qwen2.5-VL** performs strongly in Stage II reasoning quality.
- **QVQ-Max** achieves the best Stage III overall score (**11.45**), suggesting better multi-step reasoning coherence.
- Strong domain perception does **not** automatically translate into strong structured geo-semantic reasoning. 

This highlights the central motivation of Geo-BC: **captioning ability is not enough** for reliable geo-semantic reasoning.
