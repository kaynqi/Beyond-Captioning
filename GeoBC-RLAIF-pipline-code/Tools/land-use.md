## 1. Cropland

* **  Paddy Field**: Cultivated land used for planting aquatic crops (rice, lotus root, etc.), including land under rotation between aquatic and dryland crops.
* **  Irrigated Land**: Cultivated land with guaranteed water sources and irrigation facilities that can be irrigated normally in average years, used for planting dryland crops (including non-industrial greenhouse land).
* **  Dryland**: Cultivated land without irrigation facilities, relying primarily on natural precipitation for growing dryland crops.

## 2. Garden/Orchard Plot

* **  Orchard**: Land used for growing fruit trees.
* **  Tea Plantation**: Land used for growing tea plants.
* **  Other Garden Plots**: Land used for growing mulberry, rubber, cocoa, coffee, oil palm, pepper, medicinal herbs, and other perennial crops.

## 3. Forest Land

* **  Forested Land**: Arbor forest land with a canopy closure $\ge 0.2$, including mangrove land and bamboo forest land.
* **  Shrubland**: Forest land with a shrub coverage $\ge 40\%$.
* **  Other Forest Land**: Includes open woodland (canopy closure $\ge 0.1$ but $< 0.2$), unestablished forest land, harvested areas (slash), and nurseries.

## 4. Grassland

* **  Natural Pasture**: Grassland dominated by natural herbaceous plants, used for grazing or mowing.
* **  Artificial Pasture**: Refers to grassland where forage crops have been planted manually.
* **  Other Grassland**: Grassland with tree canopy closure $< 0.1$, a soil-based surface layer, dominated by herbaceous plants, and not used for animal husbandry.

## 5. Construction Land

* **  Urban Construction Land**: Land within built-up areas of cities and towns used for various types of residential housing, commerce, public facilities, industrial production, and storage.
* **  Rural Construction Land**: Land within rural villages used for residential homesteads, shops, breeding facilities, and other public facilities.
* **  Human-Disturbed Land**: Categories currently undergoing construction, production, or other human activities during the monitoring period that may cause soil erosion (e.g., mining, quarrying, projects under construction, demolition sites, etc.).
* **  Other Construction Land**: Land used for industrial production, material storage, salt pans, military facilities, scientific research, historical sites, etc., that are independent of towns or villages.

## 6. Transportation Land

* **  Rural Roads**: Within rural areas, with a width of $1.0\text{m} \sim 8\text{m}$ in the South and $2.0\text{m} \sim 8\text{m}$ in the North; unpaved roads serving rural agricultural production (including tractor paths).
* **  Other Transportation Land**: All built-up transportation land except rural roads, including **paved roads** located in rural areas.

## 7. Water and Hydraulic Facilities Land

* **  Rivers, Lakes, Reservoirs, and Ponds**: Includes rivers, lakes, reservoirs, ponds, various tidal flats, and hydraulic structures.
* **  Marshland**: Land that is frequently flooded or waterlogged, supporting the growth of hygrophytic plants.

## 8. Other Land

* **  Saline-Alkali Land**: Land with surface salt-alkali accumulation where natural salt-tolerant plants grow.
* **  Sandy Land**: Land with a surface covered by desert and essentially no vegetation (coverage $< 5\%$).
* **  Bare Soil**: Soil-based land with vegetation coverage $< 5\%$.
* **  Bare Rock/Gravel Land**: Land with bare rock or gravel coverage $> 70\%$.
